Staircase Status Mark

The Core Idea

Staircase Status

Three ascending steps (ticket priority tiers P1→P2→P3) each carrying an embedded status indicator. Read bottom-up: critical issues at the base, in-progress in the middle, resolved at the peak. The whole IT ops picture in a single mark.

Top step — P3 / Resolved. Green indicator glowing. Performance is good. SLAs met.
Middle step — P2 / In Progress. Amber indicator active. Being worked, watching the clock.
Bottom step — P1 / Critical. Red indicator lit. Tracked, escalated, owned. This is exactly what Lotris is built for.

Each light sits embedded at the right end of its step

Composition Variations
Var 1 — Lights embedded flush
Indicator circles are inset into the step's right edge. Clean, compact. The light is part of the step.
Var 2 — Lights raised above step
Indicator floats slightly above the step's top-right corner. More dimensional — like a physical status lamp mounted on a shelf.
Var 3 — Notch cut-out style
Like a physical panel indicator: the step has a circular cutout, and the light fills it. Very product, very hardware ops-room.
Var 4 — Pill steps (rounded, modern)
Fully-rounded "pill" steps feel more contemporary SaaS. Softer brand expression. Works well at small sizes.
Var 5 — Lights form a diagonal
The 3 light centers align on a 45° diagonal — creating a secondary visual rhythm that flows R → G across the mark.
Var 6 — Ultra minimal
Reduce the noise. Small simple dots on the right of each step. Maximum legibility at favicon/16px scale. The essentials only.

Discussion Points
Step direction?
Steps currently go left-aligned, descending right. We could flip so widest step is on the right, or make steps ascend bottom-left to top-right (like a growth chart).
Light order?
Currently green top / amber mid / red bottom = "resolved at the peak." Could invert to red-top meaning "top priority = most critical." Which narrative fits your pitch?
Step fill?
Steps are currently dark blue-gray except the top (indigo). We could make each step match its indicator color (red step, amber step, green step) for a bolder, more colorful take.
